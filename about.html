About Mount Everest

The Highest Peak on Earth

Mount Everest, standing at an impressive 29,031.7 feet (8,848.86 meters), is the highest mountain above sea level. Located in the Mahalangur Himal sub-range of the Himalayas, it straddles the border between Nepal and Tibet (an autonomous region of China).

Geological Marvel

Formed by the collision of the Indian and Eurasian tectonic plates, Everest continues to grow approximately 4 millimeters each year. Its formation is a testament to the immense geological forces that shape our planet.

A Mountain of Global Significance

Beyond its geological importance, Everest represents a pinnacle of human achievement and adventure. It challenges mountaineers physically and mentally, symbolizing the human spirit of exploration and perseverance.
